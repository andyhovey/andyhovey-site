Product design and direction from Aotearoa New Zealand.

I use design research and interaction design to help organisations work out which problems to solve and how to solve them. I believe complex challenges are best tackled by collaborative, multidisciplinary teams.

Since August 2019 I’ve been working with the inspiring team of scientists, engineers and communication experts at Xerra Earth Observation Institute. We’re building powerful, elegant software to help us monitor and protect Aotearoa’s maritime domain, amongst other projects focused on improving our natural environment.

From 2016 – 2019 I explored some of the world’s great mountain ranges on bicycle and foot. Very fortunate I know. Photos of mountains, like the one below, can be seen on instagram.com. I also make a cycle touring related product over at waywardriders.com.

Mountain, woman, cross — Peru, 2016

Prior to cycling touring I was the Head of Design at Xero.com, working on small business software used by millions of people around the world.

In previous roles I’ve worked on websites and apps for the New Zealand Red Cross, Klim Type Foundry, Kiwibank, New Zealand Arts Festival, Amazon and many others. More work history (and birthday notifications from people you don’t know) can be found on Linkedin.com.
